Constellations

Constellations are a group of stars forming a recognizable pattern that is traditionally named after its apparent form or identified with a mythological figure. Modern astronomers divide the sky into eighty-eight constellations with defined boundaries. The closoest constellation is the Centaurus constellation. At the moment it is the 9th largest constellation, occupying an area about 1060 square degrees. It is located in the 3rd quadrant of the southern hemisphere. More constellations include Aquila, Aries, Cancer, Cetus, Gemini, and more!
